Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace is one of South Korea’s most famous historical sites. Built in 1395, it was the main palace of the Joseon Dynasty. Visitors can explore the beautiful architecture and watch the traditional changing of the guard ceremony.

N Seoul Tower

N Seoul Tower sits on top of Namsan Mountain and offers panoramic views of Seoul. It is also a popular spot for couples to hang “love locks” and take in the city lights at night.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is a traditional neighborhood in Seoul filled with old Korean houses called hanoks. Walking through the village is like stepping back in time, with charming streets perfect for photos.

Jeju Island

Jeju Island is a volcanic island known for its stunning natural beauty. From Hallasan Mountain to waterfalls, lava tubes, and beaches, it is a top destination for outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery.

Demilitarized Zone

The DMZ is the border area between North and South Korea. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the history, see the military zones, and understand the division of the Korean peninsula.

Changdeokgung Palace

Changdeokgung Palace is a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its harmonious architecture and the Huwon Secret Garden. The garden is especially beautiful in spring and autumn, offering a peaceful escape in Seoul.
